There are arguments people choose.

And there are arguments that have been waiting silently inside them for weeks.

This was the second kind.

Her last message remained on the screen.

**I was busy.**

Three words.

Careless words. Efficient words. The kind people use when they do not understand the distance between inconvenience and hurt.

I read them again, hoping they would improve.

They did not.

For days I had imagined explanations large enough to justify silence. Family trouble. Illness. Crisis. Some dramatic interruption worthy of patience.

Instead, she had offered the most ordinary excuse in the language.

Busy.

Something in me snapped—not loudly, but cleanly.

## The First Blow

**Busy people can still reply once,** I typed.

Sent.

She responded after a few seconds.

**So now there are rules?**

**No. Just manners.**

**Wow. Happy New Year to you too.**

Her sarcasm only sharpened me.

**Don’t start acting innocent. You saw the messages.**

Pause.

Then:

**Yes.**

No apology attached.

Just yes.

The honesty of it somehow hurt more.

**And still said nothing?**

**I didn’t know what to say.**

I stared.

For weeks I had been drowning in possible meanings, and her answer was uncertainty.

## Everything Returns

Once anger finds a door, memory opens all the windows.

I typed faster now.

**You could have said that.
You could have said you were busy.
You could have said talk later.
You could have said stop messaging me.
Anything would have been better than reading and disappearing.**

She took longer to reply.

**You are overreacting.**

That word is gasoline in human form.

**Am I?**

**Yes. We are friends. Why are you behaving like this?**

Friends.

I almost laughed.

When people need closeness, they choose warm words. When they need distance, they choose definitions.

**Friends don’t make each other feel invisible.**

The typing bubble appeared.

Stopped.

Appeared again.

Stopped.

Then:

**You are making too much drama out of chats.**

I leaned back in disbelief.

Chats.

To me they had become nights, moods, expectations, breathless waiting.

To her they were chats.

## The Truth Slips Out

My anger changed shape then.

Less sharp. More honest.

**It wasn’t just chats for me.**

I sent it before fear could stop me.

There was a long silence.

Long enough for regret to arrive.

Then she replied.

**I know.**

My pulse jumped.

**You know?**

**I’m not blind, Ronak.**

The room felt suddenly smaller.

Everything became dangerous now.

Because if she knew, then what had all this been?

I typed slowly.

**Then why did you ignore me?**

Her answer came in fragments.

**Because…**

Typing.

Stopped.

**Because you were becoming too serious.**

Typing.

**And I didn’t know how to handle it.**

I read those lines again and again.

There it was.

The first real truth between us.

Not cruelty.

Not hatred.

Fear.

## The Button

But pain rarely becomes wisdom immediately.

At that age, I only heard rejection.

Too serious.
Didn’t know how to handle you.

My pride rose like a soldier late to battle.

**Then don’t handle it anymore.**

Before she could reply, I opened her profile.

The same baby picture.

The same hidden life.

The same girl who could enter my days casually and leave damage behind.

My cursor moved to the button.

**Unfriend**

I hesitated.

A final message appeared in chat.

**Ronak wait—**

I clicked.

The profile refreshed.

**Add Friend**

Done.

My heart pounded as if I had done something brave.

I had done something emotional.

Those are not the same.

## Aftermath

The chat window remained open.

Her messages could still come for the moment.

Then one arrived.

**Did you just unfriend me??**

I stared at the screen.

Part of me wanted to reverse everything instantly.

Another part wanted her to feel one fraction of what I had felt.

I typed:

**Yes.**

Sent.

**Why?**

Because I missed you too much.
Because I built too much around you.
Because your silence humiliated me.
Because I wanted control over at least one ending.

Instead I wrote:

**Many reasons. Can’t explain now.**

Her reply came quickly.

**That is rude.**

I almost smiled bitterly.

Now she had found the word.

## The Silence After Victory

After that, I closed the laptop.

The room went dark.

Outside, fireworks still cracked in the distance. Laughter still traveled through the neighborhood. Somewhere people were hugging, dancing, welcoming beginnings.

I sat alone in the dark with the feeling people often mistake for victory.

Relief mixed with emptiness.

I had removed her from my friend list.

I had not removed her from myself.

And that was the more difficult task still waiting.
