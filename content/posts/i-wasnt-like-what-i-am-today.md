The Way I Was

I wasn’t the same as I am today. I used to be the sweetest son of my parents. My
elder brother always admired me. Mom used to say they are my three sons, one
Engineer, one Doctor, and another one Professor. Well, The elder brother
fulfilled their wish. But what about me and my younger brother? I feared biology
or I didn’t like biology teachers, I don’t know the reason. Just I wanted to
discard it from my syllabus.

And the result is what I am. People used to be happy when their son or daughter
gets into an engineering college. They had the ambition of much money or
something. But now you will feel embarrassed to say that you are doing
engineering. And don’t say about IT or Computer Science Branch. I got lots of
compliments from many of the elders when I choose the CSE branch. Dad wished for
civil. But I had a special interest in Computers actually graphical designing.
But now my younger brother does better in this stuff than me.

Mom always used to praise me for my deeds. I obey mom and dad, never fight, and
do what task is given. That’s why I was the dearest among all. Although
sometimes mom used to scold me still I loved her. I was much friendlier to one
of my cousins. We play together, study together, read together, and eat
together. I was happy with the life I had. I thought of my maternal family as my
big great family. I was never attached to my paternal family. It is obvious:
because you would prefer that part of the family who loves, and care for you
much. Isn’t it right? I eagerly waited for the summer holiday so that we (Me,
Big Bro, Younger one, Mom, and Dad) could go to grandpa’s house.

It was a little bit different in school life. I was scared of going to school
until I was 6yrs. Well, I used to enjoy reading books before they are taught in
class. I can remember I was not so good in my primary school although I stood
first for these three years (1st and 2nd standard is never considered: Everyone
is passed only). I respect teachers but also there was a fear behind it. Still,
now I can’t stand before my teachers properly. I was the favorite of two of my
teachers. Now one of them is in God’s world and another one is living happily in
her married life. I had many friends (around 3 or 4) who like me also get
jealous. Surely anyone will get jealous if you get his/her position. I used to
visit the jungle, trees, and the outside fields. I am fond of playing cricket.
Even in the hot noon, I pursue my younger bro to play cricket. I prefer bowling
and if anyone of you gets my batting style then you can’t stop laughing. I am
much weaker in batting still now. I got a scholarship in the fifth class. It was
480 rupees (Rs. 20 for 2 yrs.). But after so many attempts I was provided only

1. I was in 4th class and there was a quiz competition from the human welfare
club. I stood first and one of my seniors got 2nd. He was my science teacher’s
elder son. It was only 200/-. But for that, we (I and Dad) had to go to a nearby
block by bicycle which was one hour journey. Most often when I would have some
exam or competition dad would take me there. We used to leave around 4.30 am. So
that we can be there before the 6.00 am exam.

Dad had brought up us in this way. We had to participate in competitions, and
attend classes regularly, skipping exams was a dream for us. No matter what you
do, do your best. Dad was never forced to get first in class. But he got happy
with it. Sometimes I gave surprise them. I won a compass box being first in a
debate on the occasion of Independence Day. Although I always prepared for it it
was not like that I tell everything that I read. I had to add something
different. Walking into the rally was like being Proud to be.

I felt like I was the person who was going to save all those people one day. I
was patriotic. Well, it’s also true that we used to get chocolate, biscuits or
other stuff for it. But who cares about that? I was never after candy. Actually,
I didn’t need it.

After changing schools I was a little bit nervous. But I was always among the
familiar faces. I joined upper primary school about 2 km. away from home. It was
like a habit to change home for us. We didn’t last in one house for more than 3
yrs. till now we have been in 7th rented house. I was in the 4th rented house in
my 6th class. Actually the 6th and 7th classes transformed me a lot. The
teachers were familiar; one of them was my dad’s friend. His wife was my
relative aunt who loves me as her son till now. Two of them were like my elder
sisters. So not to worry. Aunt's teacher used to sit in the middle of the class.
She taught math and Oriya. She used to solve and let us solve the math. Only for
her, I got 98 and 99 in the corresponding final exams in the 6th and 7th.
Actually, one of the ma’am who taught us history was my favorite and I liked her
a lot. You know what liking means for a 6th standard boy.

There was one of my competitors, I forgot her name. She was dad’s friend’s
daughter. I was not close to her. But my younger bro was. Till now I can’t get,
how he does these things. I was and am always formal with girls. I can’t go
zigzag. I always come to the straight point for which some people may not want
to be friendlier to me.

There I was inspired to start singing. I usually recite songs in me. One day my
aunt (teacher) listened to me and contacted to dad to let me rehearse in the
evening for the great Diwali. So I was in. I usually practice at home. Mom
encourages a lot. When she sings Bhajans in the evening I used to attend to her.
And it went that way…  Then on Diwali, it happened and I got 500/- in the prize.
Well, the way I was going was superb. I was presented with a prize for the quiz
and song again. The 7th board exam came. We were well prepared. When I was
writing for exams, I could never find my pages myself. They were always with
someone else or in the hand of some of the students. Because there was not so
much restriction in the exam hall.

For god’s sake, I got 7th position at the district level. I wasn’t so much aware
of what to be in the top ten. But dad was really happy. I was the only student
in my block. Swarup’s friends Alok Sahu and Madhumita Meher were on the lists
who were from SVM Phulbani. I knew MM because her mother was my dad’s friend.

Meanwhile, big bro got second class in the 10th board. Dad was upset and tense.
Dad was transferred to Kantamal. So we had to move to Phulbani. Big bro at Govt.
College there. Me in the 8th. Younger in 6th. Wherever I went the debate never
left me. It continued like always. Here also I got friends and competitors.
Well, I was well treated because their best student lagged 30 marks behind me.
But we were three friends, close friends. We play together, go to school
together, and do stuff. Younger bro was more likely to be friendlier to my
friends who they may be. Also my big bro. But I like the elder ones like big
bro’s friends. I went to the Diamond tutorial. Satish sir, Ratha sir, and
Srinivas sir, I can never forget their effort to bring me up to this level. I
bet there is anyone who can fight in English grammar with Srinivas sir. Satish
sir was actually a professor but he choose it. All of a sudden I became the most
wanted student in the school and tuition. Then a load of debate, quiz all these
things, Oh god.

Now entered into Scout group. Attended two Scout camps in 8th and 9th. In 8th, I
went with my dad because he was the scoutmaster for his school. I had made a
project on “safety in the jungle”. Third in Adventure Activity. Debate failed.
Of course, I expected that. But enjoyed it a lot. At school, Gopinath sir was my
favorite. Only for him, I got 730 marks on the 10th board.

There was a girl in my tuition who was pretty. I liked her. But I can understand
how we do foolish things in childhood so that we can laugh at ourselves later. I
wonder why I got connected to a girl every time although I didn’t wish. That’s
why I preferred to join a boys’ High School. Satish sir always inspired me. Ha
used to tell me how the seniors are reading, and how was their attitude. And
most of the time about English medium students. We were only 5 boys in tuition
in the 9th class. All others went in their way. We used to stay in the tuition
at night with Satish and Manu sir. They helped us in the study as well as guided
us. When the current went off, we used to start scaring each other, bark like
dogs and make loud sounds. (In the absence of Sirs). We celebrated Ganesh Puja
and Saraswati Puja with great devotion. We went on a trip in 9th class with
tuition sirs. On the occasion of Teachers’ day, me and my friends,
senior brothers, sisters all together decorated the tuition. Our +2 section
lectures were invited that day. I came home to arrange for the gift. Well, I
forgot to change my clothes and went in them. Again debate competition. No one
forced me. But how could I control myself? I was habituated to that then.

The guests were the Math Lecture, and Physics Professor and both of them were my
BADA MAMU’s friends from when Grandpa was living in G.Udaygiri. But they had no
idea of me as it was around 7 yrs. they last saw me. They only knew my Big Bro.
Actually we three brothers were reading in that same tuition.

That day I got first prize again. That day they came to know of me. I was happy.
That day photos still I have in my Album. They aren’t to be compared with any of
the professors I have ever seen or will see in the future. But this time I got
no response from the parent side for that gift. It hurt more.

To bad luck or something, Big Bro failed in +2. Guess what would have happened
to Dad and Mom. Still, they remained quiet. I wish I could ever understand them,
their feelings. My respect for them upraised. They didn’t think of what happened
but thought of what to do next. Although Mom often scolds us. But Dad, NEVER.
Now I can understand why parents are God. I often thought of praying for their
feet at least once a day. Still, I am not able to do it. I am such a loser.
Sometimes I feel disgusted being part of this family, and sometimes feel PROUD.
I think if I were in that situation what would have I done that makes me a
little bit emotional? I don’t know how to love Family. But I miss them when I am
away. I was never ashamed of being a brother of my Big Bro. He saved me twice in
childhood. Always saves and will save in the Future, I have such Confidence.
