## Chapter 1: The decision of a nerd

Being nerd, the best thing is that you don’t have to bother about the whole
world, knowledge is everything for you, the knowledge on subjects not the world,
but the worst thing is that you miss out the most amazing things around the
world. And that’s what I was. Who knew, how life would lead me to what I am? It
was merely just a matter of chance. I had never planned this nor my parents.
They sought after me to be a doctor. But they couldn’t change my mind to choose
the alternate by their persuasions. I was determined and nothing could have been
done to alter my decision. That doesn’t mean I had a clear view of my future and
profession but it was just a fascination and also fear. There was only two
alternatives one doctor for which I had to digest the giant biology book and
other one was technical. At any cost I wasn’t going to read or remember those
biology stuffs (I got bored of those from matriculation period), so I was left
with to pick the other one. That decision led me to here, to PMEC and to them
who have become an integral part of my life. Yet I can’t just miss out the life
before PMEC.

It did hurt. Second in district level. I was expecting to be the first. Although
the score was much better but still I was unsatisfied. I could have tolerated it
but just missing from what I deserved or wanted most by 4 marks was more than
just pain. My parents were happy not to such extent that they would celebrate
it. There was a cold war between schools for showing that they had the most
promising students and the topper would be from their school. And I failed my
school. To cover up my inner sorrow I went to my well-wishers and teachers with
my dad to discuss from where to peruse my +2. Some advised for BJB College BBSR,
some for other colleges. I had no idea about what should I consider. As the
diploma rank was 92, yet it was never in my option because I wanted to do +2 and
so do my teachers. After consulting with Big Bro, it was decided that I was
going to get admitted in a college in Berhampur and going to stay with Big Bro.
I had nothing to object the decision made by elders although my teachers wanted
me not to leave Phulbani.

Dad had been transferred to a new school and one of his colleague was living in
Berhampur. We took the first bus to Berhampur and we were in Mr. Mohapatra’s
house by 10am. We got freshen up and had a belly full of meal. The sir has
shortlisted some colleges and brought their application forms. I tried my best
to fill those forms up and hand over to dad. First we went to khalikote junior
college. I had never seen such a mass traffic in my whole life. I was nervous
and uneasy with this city life. I was wondering about how I was going to cope
with it. After struggling for two hours I could finally submitted the form. I
had no intention to visit the city after such a tiring wait. But sir had his
way. He called for an auto and asked to go to Nilakantha Nagar
Saraswati Vidya Mandir. He charged rs.50 for it. I really was impressed by the
surrounding and neat and clean environment. I had always dreamed of being a part
of any Saraswati Vidya Mandir. I had removed the other options when I saw the
college itself. As I was depositing the form, the clerk stared me and my
application form continuously for 2 minutes and on returning the receipt he
informed that I should have a look around the college and the facilities. I
glanced at dad to know that he was noticing me and I could see his approval.
Someone with white color shirt and trouser like a saint came near us and showed
the way into the college. The corridor, the rooms, the labs and the offices,
there was not a sign of dust or mark for years. It was being taken care of like
a new born little baby. I wasn’t too advanced to ignore all of it. We entered
into the principal’s office and took seats. He was too polite to be a principal.
I was impressed by every single word that he said but he wasn’t surprised for me
not uttering a word throughout the conversation. When the topic of joining came
out, he insisted on getting admitted there as soon as possible as he didn’t want
to let a talented student slip out of his friend, I suppose.

Back in home, when I was asked for my opinion I was way too sure about my
admission into Saraswati Vidya Mandir, Nilakantha Nagar. So after I got the
letter from college I got admitted there.

## Chapter 2 : Way to Berhampur and the lost, found  

Before the commencement of classes I had prepared myself for the very reason I
was going to be there. Still I had two days in my hand and lots of things to do.
I had bought the uniform from the prescribed cloth store, had the syllabus in my
mind, bag packed and ready to go. I hadn’t informed big bro of me coming. I knew
the area where he used to stay that was Mandia Palli, NH5, well as I had been
there for two days before when I went there for the diploma entrance exam
conducted by NTTF. I got 59th rank from 60 seats in the electronics branch but I
had no interest on that. Dad had booked a ticket in the LIZZA bus and its
departure time was 11pm. Dad dropped me at bus stand and waited until I got
disappeared from his sight. I always preferred the window seat as most of us do
and had nothing to do in that night except of enjoying the cool breeze and the
demonic view of outside at least that’s what I expected. After a five minutes
ride on the bus a lady, may be of 21years old yellowish fair complexion and
curly hair, came towards my seat and put her bag on the carrier and sat next to
me. She has a smiley face.

“Hey, where are you going?” she moved towards me and asked occasionally.

“Yes!!!!!!!!” I was shocked.

“I was asking about your journey destination.”

“Ohhh, Berhampur.”

“So I am. By the way I am Alisha. What about you?”

“Ronak ….. Ronak Bharadwaj”

“So Berhampur. For study?”

“Yes, +2 actually.”

“Ok I am not going to bore you asking about the marks, career etc. etc. but
still I can ask you what is your ambition or motive in this life?”

“Still unaware of it, didi” my tongue slipped.

How could I relate her with me by the word DIDI (sister)?

“Now as you are calling me didi, so I have this much of right to scold you for
not having any idea about your own life.”

Please don’t act like my mom.

 “But you tell me didi, what do you do and what do you want to do in your life?
I think I may ask!” I charged back.

“I am doing honors in economics and journalism is my dream.”

“Really!!! How do you sure about it?”

“You just have to listen to your heart and let it flow its way. Encourage
yourself for what you want from your heart and it will find its way to you.”

“Didi, you really are very certain in this.”

“Tell me what your heart says?” she again started the interrogation.

“As you are asking so eagerly I think I want to be a programmer, I can tell for
now.”

“But it’s tough to be persistent in software industry, you know!!!”

“Everyone says like that but I like this.”

“Hmmm… that doesn’t matter what you do, matter how you do that. What is this
book in your hand?”

I looked at my hand. I had a story book, the story of Peter Pan the boy who
defeated Captain Hook.

“Ohhh… this. It’s the adventure story of Peter Pan, by J. M. Barrie. Have you
read this before?”

“Yah…when I was a girl.”

“Means…”

“What means? I am a lady now not a girl anymore, you know.” She was stormed.

I had nothing to reply.

“You know those who reads much also can write much. Do you write anything?” She
said in an informing voice.

“No. Never thought about writing anything other than the question answers.”

“Try someday.”

“May be.” I was unsure of me and my words.

I brief silence. Again she broke the silence by saying “so close the book and
keep inside and go to sleep. It is too late.”

I looked at my watch and it was 00.15am.

“Yes. Di. Good night.” The di word came out of love and affection and I felt her
like my own sister who was lost for years. The transformation from didi to di
was very fast to my knowledge.

“Hmm… good night.” She covered her head in her shawl.

I peeped my head outside the window but return back getting unsatisfied with the
infinity dark and the never ending shadows. I didn’t know when I fell asleep.

Someone’s sweet voice interrupted me from continuing to stare my future
girlfriend whom I had never met before. It really was too sweet to let me in my
dream. I opened my eye lids forcibly who weren’t cooperating me. Yes, there she
was checking her bags and putting things into it.

“Berhampur?” I asked doubtfully. I had been barely twice or thrice to this town
before.

“Yes. SAHABJADE” she was acting like my sibling elder sister, that I dreamed of,
yet wasn’t blessed with.

I suddenly got up and packed things into my bag and sat on my seat.

“Ready?” she asked seeing me sit idle.

“Yes. Where will you get off the bus?”

“New bus stand. And you?”

“Puruna bus stand, I have to take the mini bus to Chhatrapur.”

“Why are you going to Chhatrapur?”

“Actually I am going to Mandia Palli to stay with my Big Bro.”

“Then don’t go to Puruna bus stand, just get off at State Bank Square and wait.”
After a pause she said “give me your cell phone.”

“Cell phone?”

“Just give.” She showed an angry look.

I handed over it. She typed some number and did something and returned back
quickly and showed outside that my stop had arrived. I got off and turned to say
a bye. Before I could get a chance to say anything the bus has passed by me and
only I could hear her voice unclearly and just interpreted from her lips
movement “I have saved my number in your phone. Call me when necessary. I stay
near New Bus Stand. And thank you for calling me, didi. I wanted a brother like
you. Bye.”

I was happy. As I was heading towards one of the big rock to sit, I looked at my
mobile to see “my sweet di - 9937450129”. Yes she was the sweetest di ever.

## Chapter 3 : The day of Introduction

I was waiting for the mini bus for one and half an hour. Still there was no sign
of any transport except of the giant trucks and interstate govt. buses. Yet some
of the private buses were passing by. In those moments I had been accompanied by
some street dogs and other passengers who were there for the same cause. I had
many short sleeps meanwhile. It was really hard and like a punishment for a boy
who didn’t leave the bed until 8 in the morning. I miss my home, the smoothness
of my bed and the warmth of my shawl.

Finally my ever longed waiting got fruitful and I saw a white color Tata mini
bus approaching towards us. The time it arrived the stand without a thought I
entered the bus and asked if it was going to Mandia Palli or not. The positive
indication increased my inner joy and I looked around for a window seat and got
my butt settled keeping my bag on my lap for safety what I thought. The early
morning breeze was favoring to my need of a sleep. I promised myself not to
sleep during the journey but my sleeping tendency didn’t let me keep it and soon
I was in my dreams.

“Mandia Palli…. Mandia Palli…. Mandia Palli” Someone roaring in my dream.

I was dreaming of me being at home and mom is feeding me by her hand my favorite
meal rice,dal,allu jhuluna bhaja. And was giving me advice about how to listen
to Big Bro, how to study hard, how not to take outside fast foods, when what to
cook etc etc. but I was busy watching the movie MATRIX. We had recently bought a
DVD player and Big Bro had sent some movie DVDs from Berhampur. I was watching
them day night as no tension of study at all. Suddenly the hero cried and
continued with loud voice “Mandia Palli…. Mandia Palli…. Mandia Palli”.

“What is wrong with it? How come the Hollywood script writer know about
Mandia Palli?” I asked myself in my dream.

The sudden brake impact woke me up and I realized whose loud voice I was
listening in my dream and it was worth noticing for me that the dreams had a
solid connection with the environment surrounding me and my senses. I moved my
eye around and got amazed by the zipping capacity of the bus without any quality
compressing software. The value and importance of the bus raise in my view.
Still I had no clue how to get at least a small gap to bring myself out of the
bus. Then the conductor suggested to give him the bag through window and get off
the bus in the same way. I was surprised the way he said it casually. I did the
same and gave the bus fare 5rupees. I crossed the NH5 after the fifth trial.
Before that every time it seemed I could cross any truck or bus would come
across and would step back. I wondered even in the early morning you can’t avoid
an accident here. It was 6.30am. There was a small shop nearby. I went there and
asked for some cake and a biscuit packet. Then I started walking towards Big
Bro’s mesh biting the cake and biscuits which after some time waiting in my
stomach for satisfying my hunger.

There it was. The mesh house. I knocked the door. After knocking for 3minutes
the door got opened and there was Big Bro standing in front of me still trying
to have an idea who the hell was knocking in the early morning in a Sunday. I
could notice surprise as well as shock in his face which I had imagined and
expected for.

“What the!!! How come you are here? And how come no one informed? … …. …. ” he
was unstoppable with his questions without giving me any chance to answer.

I thought better to let him ask and me being silent.

“Give a water bottle, I am thirsty” I intervened.

He gave a bisleri water bottle and I had a sip. Without letting him ask any
further I told him that I was tired and need rest. He gave me a shawl and I
slept where Big Bro might had slept before my arrival. I could barely hear Big
Bro talking over phone with Dad. I didn’t care. I was too sleepy to care about
that.

It had been 10.30am when I finally was up to my strength and out of sleep. Big
Bro had prepared something for the lunch which we took after I brushed and took
the bathe. He had his own small temple in there in his room. I prayed there
before lunch. He had the clear view of my plan and nothing to ask about. He told
me the early days of him there and his adjustment with the mates. The college
life style, the timing. We set up what to do, how to do when to do. He
introduced me all his mesh mates. They were Narayan Bhai, Subham, and Tanish
Bhai. The one who was staying with bada bhai wasn’t there. He showed the whole
house. It was actually a house for family. I had neither good nor bad opinion on
the house. I came to Big Bro’s room and arranged my stuffs. The one who was with
bada bhai, entered with a smile in the corner of his lips and hugged me. He was
up to my height.

“You are the wonder boy who is so famous in our college. Your brother is famous
for you here.” His first some sentences.

“Hmm….” I had no sentence in my mind to reply to the compliment. I had a good
impression on him by the time.

“I am Kartik… call me Kartik bhai. I am elder to you although some misunderstand
me for a 16 year boy.” I could sense his affection to me.

“Yes Kartik bhai”

It was Sunday and they had a college picnic and I was not allowed to go. I said
them that I will go around the town and would come back by evening. Big Bro gave
me a key of the dual keyed lock and left with them. I was left alone in that
place with my books and my thought that weren’t enough for distracting me from
the memories of being in home.

I love my home.

## Chapter 4 : The day of Non-Prediction

I appreciated the way Big Bro had arranged things in the room. Everything was in
its place. Everything was neat and clean. This was the quality that always made
me envy of him. Mom used to advice and scold me and my younger brother Akhilesh
giving the example of Big Bro, how he managed to keep thing in right-condition
for years, how he kept his things clean. Sometimes Akhilesh even argued with mom
that Only Manoj is your son, then why did you gave birth to us and grew us up? I
was always in a neutral. I had no objection to both of their arguments. Being
the silent listener, I had to digest both unwillingly. Then mom used to win at
last because She was a girl, woman, lady anything you can take for and they
always wins the battle how well you fight in the middle will be of no value in
the end.

15 minutes of working and I got bored. Almost 80% of my stuffs had been placed
where they should be. I looked outside to rest my eye from the usual things. I
felt like being in a village. The slow motion of the wind, the trees, the
silence, and the beauty of grass all over the land, everything there gave me an
indication of being in a village but I loved it. I could breathe to my heart. I
was free. I could dance. I could sing. I could cry. I could laugh. I could do
what I ever wanted to do. No barrier.

I took out the bicycle, locked the room and started riding to there somewhere
where I wanted to go. After 45 minutes I was in State Bank Square. I had
remembered the path till there and now was the time for research on other paths
and areas. In between I had passed by ITI, UCP and medical campus square as I
knew only these places at that time. I had no idea what kotpeta was or what
tata benz square was. I only headed to State Bank Square until I reached there.
I took the turn and soon I was in the Annapurna Market. I found a juice shop
there which is still now serving people where it was. I bought an ice cream, two
dairy milk, and drank one glass of mixed-juice. Paying the money, I was about to
go to my bicycle suddenly a bike passed by me just escaping me from a sure
accident. I was left to fell my heart bit raised and to understand the outcome
of the situation, if it would have happened which just didn’t happen. I crossed
my finger and imagined my life in these busy rush traffic without an accident.

I entered the market place with more than enough caution and I was riding my
bicycle like I had to win a slow cycle riding competition. I was looking left,
looking right, and watching the crowd. In the rush hour it was still difficult
to avoid to look at the glamour of the market, the sequentially placed shops,
well designed garment shops, the good looking ladies, stylish boys, beautiful
girls, little kids with their parents entering into shops, roaming around the
road. It was like they are in their home and the market is in their verandah.
The only thing that made me realize it not to be home was the movement of bikes,
Scotty, cycles. Cars were rarely seen.

There was a rickshaw going ahead of me really very slow even slower than me. It
troubled me every time I wanted to overtake it. Whenever I tried failed
mercilessly by the vehicles coming from the opposite side. It made me feel
insult when a boy barely 10yrs old overtook the rickshaw in a fraction of
moment. So I determined to do so. With all my energy gathered I speeded up my
pace and went right of it. I was about to get over, my phone rang and I got
distracted. To complicate the situation a car came in front from the other side
and I realized what a great mistake I had done hastily. I would have been
crushed that day if I wouldn’t have turned my way back. But still it didn’t
help. The car thrashed to the back side of my cycle. My cycle handle turned
right and crashed with a bike and got bent. I was thrown away to a little
distance on the side of the road and my face collided with a piller standing
there. I couldn’t feel myself anymore, I was drowning in the darkness. Without
my conscience I took out my phone and dialed the recent number. Soon the
blackness covered me completely and I got unconscious to find me in the bed of a
hospital.

I opened my eye and tried to understand how I was there. My head was paining,
the left leg too. There was a blooded Band-Aid wrapped over my knee. My mouth
felt different and I opened it to put my finger inside to find out that my front
teeth are broken and so was my heart. My nose and forehead was also had stiches.
The whole body was paining like I was beaten black and blue by at least 3
powerful persons. I saw a nurse coming inside. I called her and asked about my
admission into hospital. She said that some lady got me admitted there and had
gone for medicines. I was wondering who that could be. She must be some goddess
or angel to save my life. The more was the waiting to see her, the more I got
overjoyed with excitement. I searched for my phone and it wasn’t there. I had
nothing to do except of waiting for the mysterious lady.

I was resting for some while. In my dreams I could see my happy going family
when we were all together some years ago. Dad used to rule over us being the
supreme powerful person in the family. His presence in home means no TV, no
playing, stick your face to books. The moment he went out the home used to
become like a disco. Still mom had her own way of showing her dominance and
supremacy over us. Akhilesh and I were soul mates. We played together, slept in
one bed, eat in one plate except of study and wandering. When I used to keep my
mind concentrated to study books, he preferred the real life experiences. We
used to fear to even touch Big Bro’s things. They were always kept hidden and
secured from us. Big Bro had once told me “Never change yourself. Because if you
are special then only for what you are. Everyone has his/her own personality,
attitude, and behavior. You are different and that’s why people recognize you as
you. You can’t be Mahatma Gandhi or Nehru or Subhas or Lincoln but yes, you can
do the good thing they did. You can create new life style by modifying the one
you are continuing with. No more than one people can be same A to Z. this is the
nature and this is the truth. You get me, ok”. And I just stood there without
understanding what he was trying to make me understand.

I felt a smooth touch like the petals of a rose. A sweet fragrance around me
made me get attracted to it. I couldn’t be in my dreams anymore. This real-world
physical senses had control over my mind than the dream world fantasy. The
moment my eye lids opened I had to close and open them several times (I blinked)
to assure me of what I saw was real or I was still dreaming. There she was,
Alisha didi sitting beside my bed on a chair with her palm on my hand. I felt
goose bumps on my whole body. There was the medicines and fruits and juice on
the nearby table. She had the same smile on her face but this time there was
some fear and sadness mixed in it. She looked at me and asked, “Are you feeling
better now? Or I will ask for the doctor?”

“No di, I fine now. Just the pain and nothing else.” But I couldn’t resist
myself from asking her presence there and my presence too. “How did u get to
know about my accident? And how I am here?”

Her smile amplified with my most probable questions she definitely expected from
me. She replied, “Just keep patience. I am going to tell you the whole story.”

She started, “After I arrived my home I got a nap, got freshen up, talked with
some friends, had my lunch and then opened my book to study. Then I remembered
about you and tried calling you to ask your whereabouts, but you didn’t pick up.
After 4 minutes I got your call but it wasn’t you. Someone told me that you got
into an accident. I enquired about the place and reached there. The one who
called had put you onto a corner of the road safe from the rush. He helped me to
make you sit on my Scotty. You were unconscious and I asked him to come with me
but he was scared and didn’t want to put himself through this. I understood his
hesitation. I made you wrap my waist and bonded you with me using my chuni. With
a lot of trouble and hurdle finally I arrived here and the rest you can guess. I
found your brother’s number and informed him everything and he might be on his
way to here. I didn’t say anything to your parents as they may get worried
unnecessarily. It a small injury. You will be fine in two days if you take this
fruits now and also those medicines.” She sang these like a professional singer
breathlessly. My eye was wet and there were few drops of tears on my cheek. I
couldn’t bear so much love of someone for me and she did more than excellent job
to save my life in that strange unknown place. She noticed my feeling and wiping
the tears she said, “Do have I the right to help my brother in need?”

Although I didn’t say but my heart said inside, “you have right on my whole life
now. It is dedicated to you.” But just said, “I LOVE YOU THE MOST DIDI, AND WILL
LOVE ALWAYS TILL MY DEATH.” She looked bright and happy.

“Now shut up and eat. “She said in a lovely voice and gave me an apple.

I ate the fruits and drank the juice. I was about to take medicines Big Bro
arrived and got a little bit surprised seeing us both. He had no idea about
Alisha Di. He was about provoke on me for being so careless, but di explained
him everything and asked, “By the way what is your name?”

“Manoj …. “ Big Bro answered.

“AaaHan... Bharadwaj... “Di interrupted.

“The surname must have been known to you from Ronak.” Big Bro said.

After that they got engaged in introducing themselves, talked many things and so
many things that I felt like I was alone in the room and they were like I never
existed. After bearing to their uninteresting conversation I finally decided to
make them feel my presence.

“Ok guys, if your buzzing has been finished will you care to give me the
medicines?”

Both of them looked sorry for what they did. And I laughed at them. Di gave me
the tablets. I opened my mouth to take it. Big Bro shocked, “what the hell have
you done to your teeth?” but in a moment he started joking, “now I am going to
call Chandra Danta (moon shaped teeth)every time I see these broken teeth.”

That might be fun for him. But I took it seriously. From that day I never
laughed opening my mouth I didn’t want anyone to see my broken teeth and started
to smile for most of the time and most of the occasions. I couldn’t understand
that day that it was a lucky charm for me.

Bada bhai said, “I am going to talk with the doctor.” And left the room.

I asked di, “Di, which hospital is this?”

“City hospital. Why you never saw it? It is clearly visible to the State Bank
Square where you got off in the morning.” She had a mixed expression of surprise
and aware that I was new to that place.

“No I was too sleepy and bored to notice that.” I felt severe pain in my head.
”Ohhh….”

“What happened?” her reflexive reaction.

“Nothing just this headache is unbearable.”

“Let me help you.” She had the habit of helping of course.

She sat on the side of the bed and put my head on her lap. She smoothly
continued pressing every single part of my forehead. It was relaxing and caring.
I closed my eye. And the thought came into my mind if she was lucky to have me
as a brother or it was great for me to find a sister in her. The second option
was more emphatic. And I went into the usual darkness and my DREAMS.

## Chapter 5 : Not A College, A Temple of Education

I was discharged from the hospital with a lot of prescribed medicines and a
healthy diet chart. The doctor assured that I would be completely well in two
days. His confidence made me strong and I was too sure about my recovery that I
started thinking to go to college the next day. Alisha di was mixed happy and
she made a list of things to do and how not to forget to take medicines, which
medicines to be taken when etc. etc. even the doctor didn’t emphasized on it the
she was putting pressure on my innocent mind. I considered that it was all the
feminine quality to do so. Bada Bhai and I left for our room and so she did.

That night was the hardest in my life, away from home, away from mom and dad,
away from the homely environment and the day passed with a horrible event I was
overloaded. I had realized that this is the real life that I had to face. I was
prepared for it mentally, not physically. If I made a great mistake to come
here? Had I ever made any decision correct?

“Wake up, Ronak. It’s already 7. I am going for college. Take the breakfast and
lock the room properly. Bye. See you in the evening. “Bada Bhai was shouting.

I woke up. He was right. It was 7:08am. I had a little bit pain in my head and
the body wasn’t in any mood to leave the bed, that’s what we call it to sleep on
the mat covered by a shawl. Not bad either. The first thing I did to check if
Alisha di had called me or not. And here it was 12 calls and 2 messages. I
opened the message which says “good morning Ronak, why don’t you pick up the
phone. Still sleeping? Feeling well? If not take rest and no need to go to
college today. They won’t teach anything today. Call me when you wake up…”

I preferred to send a message “I am fine. Little headache. Leg is fine.”

I brushed my teeth. Ohhh I almost shocked when looked into the mirror. I had
lost my precious front teeth and it again reminded me of my promise. How ugly I
