> Conversation between 25 years girl who is an intern for a 40-year-old novel
> writer and has a crush on him

Girl: Hi, Mr. Smith. I just wanted to thank you for allowing me to intern for
you. I've been learning so much and I'm really enjoying it.

Writer: Of course, it's my pleasure. I'm glad you're enjoying it. How can I help
you today?

Girl: Well, I was just wondering if you had any advice for me as a writer. I've
always loved writing and I've been working on a novel for a while now, but I'm
not sure if it's good enough.

Writer: First of all, don't be so hard on yourself. Writing is a process and it
takes time to hone your craft. And second, don't be afraid to put yourself out
there. Show your work to others and get their feedback. That's the only way
you'll know if it's good enough.

Girl: That's great advice, thank you. I'll definitely take it to heart.

Writer: Of course. And remember, don't hesitate to ask me for help or advice if
you need it. I'm here to support you.

Girl: (blushing) Thanks, I really appreciate it. And I have to say, I've had a
crush on you since I first met you. I think you're a really talented writer and
I really look up to you.

Writer: (smiling) That's very kind of you to say. But I think it's crucial for
you to focus on your own writing and not be too concerned with what others are
doing. Just keep working on your craft and you'll get there.

Girl: (nodding) Yeah, you're right. I'll definitely keep that in mind. Thanks
again for everything.

Writer: Of course. I'm always here to help. Let me know if you need anything.

Girl: I will, thank you.

(The girl exits the room)

Writer: (sighs) She's a talented writer, but I hope she doesn't let a crush get
in the way of her work. I'll have to keep an eye on her.
