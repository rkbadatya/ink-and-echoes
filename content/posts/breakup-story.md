With a trembling heart, bent shoulders, curled hands, weakening legs, a defeated mind, and a hollowed face, he stands on the beach, staring at the limitless continuity of the sea. His mind keeps circling back to the fading memories of her. She was beautiful—even though her face now appears blurred in his mind. He can’t clearly recognize her, partly because of the sharp pain throbbing in his head as he strains to understand what’s going on inside it.

The salty, foamy white waves wash over his feet, and the wet sand beneath slowly slips away, pulling him deeper with each wave. As he continues to think of her, he watches the roaring waves crash against a giant wall of solid black rocks. A vision of her appears—she’s wearing an orange kurti and black pants, her lips glossy pink, her shiny silver-brown hair fluttering in the wind. The image becomes a little clearer, but he still can't be sure who she is.

The wind blows relentlessly, making him shiver. He starts to see her face more distinctly now—darkness under her eyes, a pale expression with a strange, gloomy calmness. She isn’t sad or angry. There’s no visible emotion—just a silence that feels like it’s been there forever. She walks toward him, saying something he can’t make out, and gently holds his hand. Her eyes shift to something shining on her ring finger—a ring with a heart-shaped stone. She removes it and places it on his hand.

The wind changes direction as she does. Then she turns and runs toward the sea. She keeps going, deeper and deeper into the water. He starts to feel moisture on his cheek—his own tears falling silently to the ground. His heart pounds harder. He’s uncomfortable and terrified, knowing she’s heading into certain danger. He tries to move, to go after her, but his body won’t respond. Not even an inch. The wind, the waves, the sand—none of them are helping him.

He watches helplessly as the water rises to her neck, her nose, her head. He screams for help, but no one hears—not even himself. Soon, he can see only her hair, and then—nothing. No trace of her remains.

Suddenly, he realizes he's sinking. The sea is pulling him in. He can’t breathe, can’t move, can’t feel anything except the pounding of his heart. He sees only darkness now. It’s closing in, wrapping around him.

The ring on his hand starts heating up. It melts, turning into small pearl-like pieces. He tries to hold on to them, but they slip through his fingers and vanish into the deep.

He feels sadness, fear, and suffocation all at once. He’s losing control, fading into an unknown world of darkness. He can’t keep his eyes open any longer. Everything becomes a blur.

Then—he passes out.
