“Just five marks. What were you doing in the exam? You couldn’t even attend all
the questions. Why this irresponsibility? I didn’t expect this from you. You
ashamed yourself. You are just disgrace.” Mam continued scolding like this for a
half an hour. She had reasons to do that. Though I was in the best ten list in
the first monthly test but secured 5 out of 15 in chemistry in the next one
which was unlike the 15 out of 15 in the first one. I even couldn’t understand
myself what could had been the reason. May be it was because I wasn’t studying
or the IIT Coaching extra works, or unhandled schedule or I was in love with my
Chemistry Mam for which I wasn’t concentrating in her class, her mere existence
was disturbing my heart, my mind was liquefying and getting adsorbed into her.
It was silly how I was taking things seriously that even I had thought about if
she would change her surname for me after marriage or not, how it would sound
when she will be addressed as Sunanda Ronak Bharadwaj or Sunanda Sahu Bharadwaj.
Yes it seems so strange but I was really very amateur, and hence it worth it.

Nothing seemed right after letting myself down before my heart, she was my heart
and every beat in it. Instead of feeling burned by the sharp indigestible words
of her I was calm and stiff. Being in the busy hour classroom crowd, I was all
alone. There was a dead silence in me. And that made me temporary deaf to listen
what the remaining classes were taught about. It was raining outside, heavily.
The weather was not so much of interest but gloomy and so was my mood. When
nothing works out, then just let yourself go. That’s what I thought about then
and let myself do what I wanted to do. I opened the back page of my notebook
which is mostly used for rough work and full of calculations and other stuffs, I
let my pen flow on the paper. I had nothing in my mind but only knew that I was
doing something. And I had to do anything. After five minutes it was a mango
like shape. 10 minutes some improvements. I had already made a face, may be
looked like my mam. It went on like that and after giving the finishing touch I
had the sketch of the beautiful lady who had burst on me for some reason that I
didn’t felt worth.

I came out side of the classroom after the ongoing class finished and went to
the corridor. I looked at the sky and then closed my eye. I spread my hand
holding the paper towards the rain and let it get wet. When I opened my eye,
there was no sketch anymore, just a wet paper. I threw it and felt relieved. I
had nothing to do except of that to make myself easy. It felt good that I was
not angry on her anymore, thought I wasn’t before also.

It was the returning time. The usual going in the mini bus. As I entered the bus
I opened my rain coat out of body, folded it and kept in the bag. I got a seat
vacant and sat there. It was a side seat but not the window one, it was the
middle ones. The seat was wet too. I couldn’t avoid that type of circumstances
in that season anyway. I had to sit there. It was almost getting darker as the
clock said 6.30P.M. The bus started and halted at the state bank square. A lady
entered and sat just in front of my seat. I could see her from her left side
almost. She had been drenched in rain completely. Her open hair was wet too and
it was seductive. I could never saw her full face but I just imagined my mam in
that situation. How would she look in sari, all wet in rain sitting in front of
me just like this? The rain drops crawling down from her forehead to the cheek,
then the wet lips, then the chin, neck and so on, finally touching the ground.
It was shivering and heart shaking moment. I wished I could be that rain drop,
that sari she had worn which was hugging her so tightly revealing her perfect
curves. I wished everything I could be that belonged to her. But wasn’t that
just a thought? Just my day dreams? I hadn’t even confessed my love to her
forget the rest. 

The police siren was blowing. To add more fun to my troublesome day, the evening
had brought me a gift, the strike at the UCP Junction. For that reason the buses
were taking bypass, but to hell with it, that route was also jammed and we had
to return back to Berhampur. Now was the time for me to get worried and I was
worried. The bus stopped dropped me at the BIjipur square. I was suggested by
the conductor to get going straight till the petrol pump and take right turn to
the state bank square. After that I am on myself how to spend the night and
where to, may be lodge. My luck was not much worse after all as by god’s grace
Alisha di called to ask my whereabouts and if I reached at the mesh when I was
near the khalikote junior college.

“Ronak, have you already arrived at the mesh?” Di asked.

“No Di. You must have known about the strike.” I replied while walking. 

“Yes. So what happened?”

“We couldn’t pass the UCP.” And I told her everything in detail.

“Then come to my home. It’ll be fun tonight.” I could hear her voice excited.
“First tell me where you are, I will pick you up.”

“I am near the khalikote junior college. I am going towards the state bank
square. I will wait for you there.”

“Ok.” I heard her telling about me to someone nearby. And I hanged up the phone.

Around 5 minutes I arrived at the state bank square. I went near the bus stand
and looked around. It was still raining but not heavily. My phone ringed. I
received the call and there she was on the other side.

“Look to your right.”

I looked right and there she was, a little wet sitting on her pleasure, one leg
on the leg base and another on the ground looking straight to me and smiling
wickedly. I cut the call and went to her. She turned her Scotty and told me to
sit on the back. I did that as an obedient student. She started it and was
riding it ferociously. I got terrified first but later it was all cool. I
couldn’t understand why she wasn’t wearing a raincoat. We both were now bathing
in the rain. The rain was spraying on me due to the wind flow. I felt cold as I
was constantly getting drenched in rain for half an hour but it seemed like she
was enjoying that.

“So how does it feel to get wet in rain?” she asked loudly as it was very much
impossible to hear due to wind and rain and the traffic. 

“It is fun but until I don’t get sick.”

“Don’t worry. I won’t let that happen to you.” She was confident.

“I know di.”

à

We arrived at her home. The house was really big and I analyzed and concluded
that she was from a rich family. There was some space in front of her house for
gardening and to keep the vehicles where she kept her Scotty. I got off and
stood there without any idea what to do. She came to me after parking the Scotty
and hold my hand and pulled or I would say almost dragged into the drawing room.
Her grip was so tight like some police holding a thief not to let go. There a
middle aged lady came. She was as beautiful as her daughter and had an angelic
smile. She came near and told,

“You must be Ronak. Alisha used to say about you. Come sit.” And she turned to
Alisha Di and provoked on her, “when will you start listening to me? I told you
to wear the rain coat but no. why would you listen to me? Look at you, all
drenched.”

“Please mom. I am sorry. I was in hurry. I am not drenched at all. Look at
Ronak, he has been bathed by rain. If I would have been a little late then he
would have been all drowned.”

We all laughed. She made the situation so light and funny. Her mom gave me a
towel to dry myself. Alisha di said me to wait there and went inside to a room
just right of the drawing room. Might be it was her room. Aunty went into
kitchen. I wiped myself and dried as well. A few minutes later Akisha di came
outside in her night dress and had brought some clothes with her.

“What is your measurement?”

“What Di?”

“See I have brought you my T-Shirt and pant. Looking at you I think it will fit
you.”

“No Di. These are not necessary. I am fine in my clothes and they will be dried
out very soon.”

“Are you fool? Take it and change. You will catch cold and get sick if you
won’t. I doubt if my clothes will be loose to you.” She grinned.

I took the T shirt where a tag line was written. “Life is just a word until you
find someone to give it a definition” and the trouser or pant which was all
yellow with green strips on it. There were two panda on two side of the pant
near the thigh area. Well I had no choice.

“Go to my room and you can change there.”

“No DI, I will prefer bathroom.”

“Ok fine. Then go straight and turn left. There it is.”

I went into the bathroom. It was big really big and there was a TV inside too. I
wondered who would keep a TV in the bathroom. We bought one TV after so many
controversial fight in our home. The inside was so neat and clean but I smelled
the Detol odor there. I changed my clothes. Di’s T shirt was few inches loose to
me but I adjusted with pant as its elastic on the waistline was closely fitted
well. Now I was confused about what to do with my wet clothes which was my
college uniform except the inner wearers. Someone knocked at the door.

“Ronak, have you changed? Keep the wet ones there. I will take care of that.”

“No Di. I will wash this.”

“I swear to GOD, Ronak if you touch them I will kill you. Come outside now.” I
was shocked hearing her saying that.

I came outside and she entered into. After five minutes she came outside with
washed and cleaned uniform in her hand. She put them in the dryer. And came to
me.

“Look how thin you are? You are looking so cute in this dress though.” She
mocked.

As I sat down on the sofa in the drawing room, I looked around. It was well
decorated and beautiful room. There was a big photo hanging on the wall where
Alisha di and a kid were with her parents. I saw Di’s father for first time. He
was middle aged person, not so fair. He had beard covered all over his face
looked like he was wearing a mask. That kid was of around 10years old but Di had
never told me about him before.

Alisha Di took my bag and took out everything inside of it. Di was sitting just
beside me. As she saw my rain coat, she looked at me surprisingly and asked
about it.

“Sorry Di. I had forgotten. Because I was worried about where to stay tonight.”
I could certainly not say her that I couldn’t remember about it as I was
dreaming about my chemistry mam. Di kept all my book and notes on the nearby
table and hanged the bag for drying it. Aunty came with two glasses of milk and
gave to each of us. While drinking the milk I asked about the photo.

“Di you never told me about your brother. Where is he? I can’t see him
here.” Di’s facial expression changed. She got faded. I suspected something bad
looking at her gloomy face.

“Never ask Mummy or papa about this ever ok. I will tell you after dinner about
it.” She got upset but I didn’t know the reason. 

In the meanwhile Uncle returned and I could see a middle aged man fully matched
to the photo on the wall but some few changes like the hair turned white and
lower belly part had emerged out very far. Di helped uncle with his office bag.
Looking at me he was surprised to some extent but without spending any second di
told him, “Papa, this is Ronak. You must have remember, I was talking about
him.”

“OH. Yes. I remember. I had imagined him little differently but he is very cute
and adorable.” He was genuinely saying that I could see in his facial
expression.

“Papa, he is not a girl.” Di said.

“I am just saying. Ok. I am going to get freshen up. You guys get ready for
dinner.” He said and went inside.

“Wait for a second.” Di said to me and followed uncle with the office bag.

As I was checking my books I got a call from badabhai and I explained my
situation. He told me to be safe and call him in the morning before leaving for
college.

I looked at the Photo on the wall again. Di had a perfect family, Uncle, Aunty,
one brother and her. It was actually a cute family. Both of the brother and
sister were looking exact same. I wondered about that boy, I mean Di’s brother.
Where he could be? May be studying away from home in some hostel, or had gone to
his uncle’s home for holidays or many more but I couldn’t have any conclusion. I
too had a happy family until badabhai left to here and after him I came leaving
mom and younger bro there, as dad mostly stay at his work place rather than in
Phulbani. Di’s calling distracted me and I came into reality. She hold my hand
and did what she knew better to do, pulled forcibly to the dining table. Uncle
had taken the head seat which was usually the one in the front breadth part. Di
sat on the left to uncle and made me sit next (left) to her. I couldn’t find
aunty there, might be she was in the kitchen. The plates were kept upside down
and there were two spoons for each plate. The table capacity was six members at
a time. A light green colour cover was fixed onto it. There was a big pigeon
flying towards the sky image was stamped on the cover perfectly in the middle of
it.

After a little while aunty came with the food and water, one item after another.
There were five items to my guess which soon revealed to be as rice, dal, paneer
khurma, alu bhindi and papad. Aunty sat in front of Di and started serving Uncle
while Di’s served for me and for herself. That question about her brother was
still haunting me, but I didn’t ask anything about it.   

Meanwhile, Uncle asked me, “So Ronak, what a pleasant surprise tonight. We are
having dinner with the most adorable brother of Lisha.” Lisha!!! Was that Alisha
Di’s nick name? Not bad.

“No uncle, I just got stuck in the city tonight due to the Strike thing going
on.”

“Oh, yes. I heard about it. It was all over the news.” Uncle remarked and asked,
“Tell us about you, Ronak.”

I wondered if that was an interrogation or just formal askings.

Di intervened, “Ronak, is this much enough for you? If you need more, ask me,
ok.”

I looked at my plate. What was that? It was completely filled with rice, dal and
bhindi fry. I hardly could digest so much.

“Di, this is more than enough. I can’t eat so much. Please Di, take out half of
it.”

Aunty said,”Ronak, you should eat that all. You are so thin and cute. People
will misunderstand you as a girl if you won’t eat sufficiently and become
healthy like a young man.” And she smiled.

And adding to her, Di said, “You know Ronak, mom has only two important thing to
do. One, to feed me healthy food and make fat like her and to celebrate my
marriage in a grand way. You know I was so fat in my school days and I was
called as Piggy-Wiggy. But After school, I made myself fit and slim and the day
I told mom about losing 20kg, mom almost became senseless. That day was her
greatest sorrow.”  

“Really! Di, were you so fat? But first, take some rice please.” And I took some
out of my plate and served on Di’s plate. She looked at me in angry eye. But I
made a little puppy face which dissolved her anger instantly.

“You have so cute face, Ronak. I can’t even get angry on you, my sweet brother.”
And pinched on my cheek.

“Yes he has. I told you before, adorable.” Uncle smirked. Everybody smiled.

Now as the dinner had been served, Uncle led us. Di told me to close eye and sit
in the Pranam posture. It was the most famous gesture to show our humbleness and
regard to the GOD while praying. Uncle started and we followed her. I could
hardly understood it or pronounce it but it wasn’t that hard either. So finally
we started eating.
