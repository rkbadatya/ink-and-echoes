I was going to Bhubaneswar to pick bada bhai up from there as he was coming by
the Rajdhani express which was to arrive there around 5:30 pm. I arrived at the
Berhampur railway station and it was still some time for Vishakha express to
arrive there. So I was just walking on the railway station platform no. 1. Then
my eye noticed a girl, beautiful of course was waiting there for some train. She
was worried and hurried. She seemed familiar but I couldn’t remember when and
where I had seen her. I wanted to talk with her or spend some time until the
train arrives, but my attitude didn’t let me do that. I moved here and there but
couldn’t gather the courage to approach her and initiate a quality conversation.
Nothing came into my mind and it was left blank with none other than the picture
of her and the fight of mind with memory cells to recover the hidden parts.
Rather an inner fear developed inside me and asked me what she would think if I
start talking with her and why would she talk with me, a stranger to her. I was
nervous and too excited. My heartbeats picked up speed. What should I ask? Then
I sat on the seat just nearer to her and constantly continued watching her every
bit of activity. She was indifferent, the same worriedness was still in her
movements. She was looking at her watch and the railway track alternatively. She
had a college bag hanging from her left shoulder to the below waist on her back.
She had loosely tied a green color ribbon on her silky freshly washed hair which
was hanging from the back part of her head through her right shoulder to the
front. She was elegant and dazzling due to the sun rays reflecting from her face
being confused about which colors to reflect and which ones to absorb. After 20
rehearsals to impress her from the first talk, I stood up. My heartbeats touched
their limit. I stepped towards her ignoring what people would think or what
would be the consequences. I stopped at a 30cm distance from her right side but
she didn’t notice me. All my words had been blocked somewhere near my throat. I
felt like being choked. I stood there for at least 2 minutes. And now she turned
her head and I was sure about it that she had a slight glance at me. it eased my
task and I suddenly asked her, “do u know what time the Vishakha express arrives
here?” she looked surprised. She stayed silent for a moment. Then said,
“really!! You wanted to ask this question?” a lot of answers were revolving
inside my head like, “of course not. I want to know about you, your name,
address, likes, and dislikes. I want to say that you are the most beautiful and
charming personality I have ever seen. Can we be friends?” but the words came
out of my mouth were, “no. I was wondering if I am late or if the train is.” She
smiled but said nothing in reply. I couldn’t understand if she liked it or not.
She smiled meant she liked it but she kept silent meant she didn’t want to talk
further. But I was not in any mood to miss the opportunity. Someone’s words were
banging on my ear, “talk with a lot of girls.” And I was doing the same. I asked
her, “are you going somewhere?” she replied, “what do you think? Why I am here?”
I got it that she was in a jolly mood and wanted to continue the conversation. I
said, “I think you are lost and got trapped here and waiting for someone to
rescue you.” She smiled again and replied, “Well, I am sort of trapped. But not
here, in my home. And rescue…it’s also true. I know someone will come one day to
take me with him far away from here.” I was surprised by the way she took my
comments positively. She seemed like known me for ages. I liked her personality
and way of expressing things, and asked, “How can you be trapped in your own
home?”

"I don’t know if u can understand." She said in a sensitive voice.

"Try me. U won’t regret it." I was trying to be as friendlier as I could be.

"Don’t know if I should say this or not." Again that confusion. I don’t like
this part of girls much where you have to convince them something which is
already understood.

"I would rather say u should definitely. We are not strangers anymore after this
8-minute talk though I would rather ask your name first. Can I?” I chose to be a
bit more conclusive and well-mannered. 

"You think so! I think we were never strangers. Well, then Ankita and I can say
u that u don’t know me." When she said that we were never strangers, it created
a series of questions but I didn’t take it into account as a part of the
discussion.

“That makes sense because u are not allowing me to know u better and I am not an
intruder to get involved in your life without your wish and dig out every secret
of your”

She responded, “Yes u r not but u know how to make someone say what u want to
listen from them”

“Now u r accusing me of something which I am not capable of” I sounded
complaining about my innocence.

“Btw I feel like saying so it’s up to u how to tackle the situation in which I
am now”

“Then I am eagerly waiting for that so u should start then” I expressed.

“You know the main problem that a girl faces after she is 21 is marriage and I
am sort of in that type of problem but a little bit complicated. After +2 dad
wanted me to be a doctor for which I had to prepare for medical but I couldn’t
qualify. Without any choice, I joined Vignan in the electrical branch without my
will, only for my parents. My poor performance implied that I am not made to be
an engineer and I decided to discontinue my study.”

“Well I think u did a pretty good job but why did u wait so long for it”

She smiled or I would rather say smirked. Again silence. This silence was
killing me.

“It’s not that simple. I told my mom and dad about my decision but then the real
problem started. They repeatedly tried to convince me what was good for me and
what was not. They had already spent a lot of money on me and my younger
brother. They have a lot of expectations from us. ”

“So it’s the conflict between what u need to do and what u want to do. But I
don’t think that this is such a big problem that u r leaving home.”

“Who said u that I’m leaving home? I am just taking a break from this
complicated messed-up life here. I haven’t said u the main reason for taking
this step yet.”

“I think we have much time to talk about it until one of our trains comes.”

“You needn’t listen if u don’t like It.” she made it clear.

“I didn’t say anything about not liking. Even I am curious about you. But
something that troubles my mind now is what it is you are so passionate about
that u are prepared to leave something which will fetch you bread and butter and
can give you a better life.”

“You are shrinking the scope to earn money. But I believe in doing something
which inspires me, brings life in me, and makes me happy and complete.”

“I am impressed by your thinking and way of enjoying life with a dedication to
what u want to do which u haven’t told me yet. Are u just testing my patience?
Cuz I am impatient about some things. ” I was really being intolerant about her
mingling talks.

“I love this nature and every creation in it. I get involved in them and love to
represent them in some pen and paper, making them alive again in my hand.
Basically, u can call me a painter but I see myself as an engineer of nature. I
would rather choose to die getting dissolved in my painting than marry someone
who doesn’t understand me, who doesn’t respect and appreciate what I do.”

“So here is the reason. U r being pushed to get married to someone who has a
completely opposite personality.” I made a conclusion.

“When I stuck to my decision to choose painting as my profession they
conditioned me to prove myself within 3 months otherwise I won’t have a choice
other than marrying with whom I can barely spend a moment.”

“This is not fare. They can’t push u onto this.” I tried to support her.

“They can. I don’t blame them. They are worried about me but can’t understand my
thoughts, and ambitions. They want stability in life but I want to risk it, the
excitement and fear to feel alive. I want to be a free bird. I want to do
something on my own”

“Looks like your vision is clear about your ambition and aim” I was enjoying
being with her and with every passing second I was falling in love with her
personality.

The long-lasting blowing sound of the train horn distracted my attention and
hers as well. She came out of her dreamy emotions. To my luck, it wasn’t
Vishakha express but to my misfortune, it was her train. She, while getting into
the train smiled at me and said, “I hope you can figure out something for my
problem”

“How can I? Even if I get one, I can’t let u know it as I don’t have any of your
contacts. I guess now u can give me your cell no so that I can give some proper
solutions.”

“No need for that you can send me a friend request” and the train left.

I thought, “that's also a great idea. But what’s her full name? Did I ask? Nope.
Then how will I get to know her on Facebook?” I was being foolish and she was
yet in my mind,” I will figure this out.”

I saw Srihari on the platform and approached him thinking that I wouldn’t be
alone during my journey anymore hoping that he too was going to Bhubaneswar.

“Happy new year 2014” was her message. I saw it and thought for a while. “What
am I going to do?” I was in dilemma. “Should I reply to her? Should I wish her
back? There is nothing bad in that, is it?” I replied, “thanks and Happy new
year…J to you too” I had nothing to lose by wishing her and showing her a smiley
face yet I didn’t expect a message from her.

It was too hard on my part to unfriend her all those happened between us. You
don’t need the courage to make friends with someone, you just have to spread
your hand of friendship. But bravery comes when you have to let someone go out
of your life when he/she is so much involved in your life and you are so much
habituated with him/her.

There were certain things that made my new year the most wonderful one ever. New
friends, a lot of friends, parties, enjoyment, letting myself go out of control,
freaking out, and of course wishes more than anything. So I was definitely in
the mood. The small flower of happiness was blossoming and turning towards the
sunshine.  The sun was brighter than ever but it was cooler. The skin was
burning with overflowing sensations. The blood was rushing inside at an
unbelievable speed. The heart was not in control, it shouldn’t be. The eye was
scattering its vision everywhere to find what it was missing. Yes, it was
missing those fighting conversations. It was missing those upsetting replies. It
was missing those waiting for replies that never came. It was missing those
attitudes which were new, surprising but fascinating. And mostly it was missing
her smile that never seen. It was missing those voices that it never heard. It
was missing those unexpected thoughts that she never knew. It was her who was
being missed most, more than anything.

“Did u just unfriend me??” she asked.

“Yes, I did. And sorry for that.” I replied with no intention of being polite.

“But why?” she wanted to know the reason.

“There are many. But I can’t say now.” I wanted to tell all of the reasons but
my ego didn’t let me do that.

“Why can’t you say?” she was insisting.

“It’s a new year. I don’t want a fight on such a happy moment.” It’s better to
leave her to figure things out on her own.

“Why will there be a fight? How can you be so rude to me?” she said.

“Am I rude? Is this what you think of me?” I was getting angry with myself for
replying to her back.

“If so then just explain it. What on earth made you unfriend me.”

“Don’t start over again. If I start, there won’t be an end to it.”

“You know what, just say it. I am here. Take time and burst out.”

“Oh, so you have a lot of time now. But where were you that time when I was
sending you messages over and over but you never replied to me? Every time I
check my inbox it says only seen at 1:30 pm or 4:12 pm etc. didn’t you ever
think about what I was going through? ” I started on.

“Then” she replied. My anger touched its limit. Just a word in reply. I didn’t
know what was in her mind, what she was doing. If she was laughing at me or
taking my words seriously. I was just opening up all those suppressed things out
of my heart unaware of its effect on her.

“I thought I was irritating you. Whenever I was looking at your profile, I just
lost myself. I couldn’t control my urge to talk with you, always forever. It was
unbearable and I was losing my concentration on everything. I started avoiding
my friends. I started to stay at home without talking with anyone, being alone
in my room, staring at my PC screen on Facebook our last conversations. I was
trying to figure out what made it so complicated. Your face was always in my
head. It didn’t let anything enter my world. And you weren’t replying. I did
what I thought was better at that moment. ” I started crying.

Every drop of tear was telling the story of every single moment I was away from
her. Every unsuccessful trial of forgetting her. Every thought of resetting my
memory to the state before I met her. Every foolishness I had done for her. Was
she worth that? Or I was getting overwhelmed. If I was stupid or she was making
me thoughtless. If I was into she or she had made me crazy. She was different
when we first met at the Berhampur Station. She was so charming, simple yet
incredibly beautiful in her nature, manner, and way of life. Her surprise entry
into my life. Those wonderful two weeks with her.

 

“You know what it would have been better if we would have never met.” I was not
regretting meeting with her that day. In fact, she made my days enchanting
afterward.

 

“No, I won’t agree with you. You were destined to meet me.”

“No, it was a silly boy’s decision. And it is better for both of us to live our
lives separately on our own.”

 

“I liked that silly boy and also this intelligent young man who is insisting to

loved that.

 

“Do you know how I spent that night after meeting you? I was happy for the

which made my life different than it was supposed to be.
