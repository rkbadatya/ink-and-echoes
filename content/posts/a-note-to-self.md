I have everything that I wanted but still there is a feeling that something is
missing. I buy what I like. After every buy I kind of feel I need something
more. Lately things are not happening as per plan. Atleast my plan. Anything I
think of the opposite happens. I am not able to fulfill my expectations. Or if I
may say God or fortune doesn't want me to have what I want. Things are happening
very slow. Also I might be doing things incorrectly. I am stuck between ideation
and prototype phase. The ideas I have or you can say the Todo list, I am unable
complete it. I'm loosing confidence on my capabilities. I am stuck between
intellij idea, vs code and eclipse. I am not getting what I want, and I don't
like what I have. But if I reflect on the words above it's all about me. Does
that make me selfish. I used to write journals about fictional characters with a
mix of reality. I had a blog you know. It all has come down to nothing. Now I'm
into upgrading this, learning a new language or build tool. But it's making me
frustrated. The laptop has less storage left. Email is not organised. We are
using old database. I want to explore a lot of things but it's not possible in
office laptop. Personal laptop is broken and it doesn't have enough power to
support that level of coding. I noted down, did research on what are the good
application to write story. Installed but didn't use. That feel isn't coming. I
am waiting for Dell XPS 17 but it's a long wait. Xps 15 is set to launch on 8th
July on Amazon. But I like the 17 inch screen and spec. I have a plan to have
own office where I can work without getting disturbed. Also once out of office
no work related things. I will read a lot of book. I'll learn Morse code. I'll
learn a language. I have heard learning a new language increases your IQ. Loved
ones around me are disappointed as I have lost the passion of writing, singing,
reading. Now most of my time is spent on social networking, youtube, office
work, buying new things. The enthusiasm of communication is simply missing.
Should I not appreciate who has helped me in the time of need? Should I not give
them my love back, show that I care, do something specially for them?

Birthdays used to be very special occasion. We were planning, preparing for it.
Waiting for friends and family's wishes. There used to genuine friendship.
Getting dressed for a festival, celebrate it the way we could, help each other
cooking, cleaning, preparing. It's used to be fun. It seems the more higher I go
on the ladder the less interesting life becomes. The less fascinating I am
becoming. The less attractive things become for which I used to die for. Over a
period of time, that feelings changes. That thrill of going out on a ride
becomes mundane. Priority shifts towards laziness. Life is bitch. It punches you
on the face when you had just thought that now everything will be all right. Few
days before I got to know Bhutni will join my office by September last week.
Today I was informed that the hiring decision has been put on hold again. I mean
WHAT THE FUCK. What do you want you life ? I mean why do you show the trailer
and tell the movie is never gonna release. Adding salt to wound, her current
company is giving half salary. And the timing is also odd. It creates an
imbalance in work life. Plus she isn't listening to me about career change. A
lot of opportunities are there outside of this testing profile. And a developer
is preferred and gets more salary.
