> A conversation between a young intern and a novel writer where she is giving
> hints to him to ask her out but he does not understand those hints.

Intern: So, what do you like to do in your free time?

Novel Writer: I love to write, of course. And I also enjoy reading, going for
walks, and trying new restaurants.

Intern: That sounds fun. I love trying new restaurants too. Have you been to any
good ones recently?

Novel Writer: I haven't had much time to go out, but I've heard great things
about a new Italian place in town.

Intern: Oh, that sounds amazing. We should go together sometime.

Novel Writer: Sure, that would be great. We'll have to make a plan for that. We
can discuss our favorite books and writing techniques over a nice meal.

Intern: (thinking she is being asked out) That sounds perfect. How about this
weekend?

Novel Writer: (not realizing she's being asked out) Oh, I'm sorry, I can't do
this weekend. I have already one commitment.

Intern: (disappointed) Oh, okay. Maybe another time then?

Novel Writer: (still not realizing) Yeah, definitely. Let's plan something for
the future.

Intern: (getting the hint) Okay, I'll keep that in mind.

Novel Writer: (still not understanding) Great, I look forward to it.

The intern hints to the novelist that she is interested in going out with him,
but he is not picking up on them. She suggests going to a bookstore and trying a
new restaurant together, but he does not seem to understand that she is asking
him on a date. The intern is clearly interested in the novelist, but he is not
responding in the same way.
