By then, he had quietly become my oxygen.

The stories, the 4:00 AM chats, the thrill of hiding under my blanket with a keypad phone—it all merged into a routine I didn't know how to live without. I didn't realize when checking for his name stopped being a choice and became an expectation.

Every morning, the first thing I did was check my messages. Every night, I waited for that one last "ping" before I could finally close my eyes.

He had a strange way of appearing at exactly the right time. Whenever I was bored in a lecture, he would message. Whenever a lab experiment went wrong and I was upset, he would somehow make me laugh.

But one night, while we were chatting casually, the tone shifted. The air in my "blanket tent" suddenly felt very thin.

"Can I ask you something?" he sent.

I smiled, my thumbs flying over the keys. "Since when do you ask permission?"

He replied instantly: "Since the question became dangerous."

I laughed to myself. I thought he was just being his usual poetic, dramatic self. I sent back a joking "Go ahead."

Then came the next message.

"Are you single?"

I stared at the glowing screen. The "Click-Click" of my phone buttons stopped.
Why today? Why this question?

I didn't feel like answering. I felt a strange pang of guilt, like I had been caught doing something I wasn't supposed to. I ignored it for a minute, watching the time on my phone screen change. Then another message arrived.

"You skipped the question."

I bit my lip. There was something restless about his curiosity. I couldn't dodge it anymore.

"Fine. At least tell me if I should stop being curious," he added.

So finally, I typed it out: "No. I have a boyfriend."

I watched the screen. He stayed online. The "Online" status was like a heartbeat. I waited for a joke, a "Haha, okay," or a change of subject.

Instead, he sent: "Are you serious about him?"

And before I could even blink: "I mean... will you marry him?"

I frowned. I was twenty years old. Marriage felt like something from a distant planet. I felt a flash of annoyance—what did this have to do with our friendship? Why was he making it so heavy?

I replied sharply: "Of course. If I have a boyfriend, then I will marry him only."

This time, there was a long, dead silence.
No joke. No follow-up question. No "typing..." sign.
Then finally, one last message:

"That is good then. Good night."

That was all. But the words felt cold. It felt like a door had been slammed shut in a quiet room. I kept staring at the chat window, waiting for one more message to fix the mood, but the green light next to his name turned grey.

The next morning, the first thing I did was check my phone.
No message.

He wasn't online. I told myself he was just busy with college.
The whole day passed. Nothing.
The next day, again nothing.
And the next.

Talking to him had become an addiction I couldn't break. I found myself opening Facebook again and again, hoping to see his name. Sometimes I opened the app without even realizing it. I'd stay there for minutes, pretending I was looking at other posts, but I was just waiting for him to appear.

But he didn't. And I was getting angry.
Why should I care so much? Who was he to me? We were just friends, weren't we?

Finally, unable to bear the silence, I decided to message him first. I opened his profile, my heart beating fast. I was ready to say "Hey, why are you being so weird?"

I froze.

Where the "Message" button used to be, there was now a button that said "Add Friend."

I felt a wave of heat hit my face. I refreshed the page. Again. And again.
But the truth remained the same.
He had removed me.

I felt a mix of anger and sharp, stinging hurt. We had become such good friends. We had shared 4:00 AM secrets and BSNL message packs. How could he just... delete me? Was it because of what I said? Was leaving quietly easier for him than explaining?

I tried to convince myself I didn't care. Who is he? Just some boy who writes stories. It shouldn't matter.

But it did matter. I missed the excitement. I missed the "ping." I even started reading our old chats, over and over, trying to find the exact moment everything broke.

A month passed. Life moved on outside the hostel. Inside, something in me was still waiting.

One afternoon, I was lying in bed, shivering with a fever and a terrible headache. I had no energy to even move my arm. My eyes were half-closed when suddenly, my phone vibrated on the mattress next to my ear.

I picked it up lazily, my vision blurred.
Then, I sat up so fast my head spun.

A message from him.
