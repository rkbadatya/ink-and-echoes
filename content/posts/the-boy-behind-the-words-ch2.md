Friendship is a strange thing. It doesn’t always happen in person; sometimes, it happens one SMS at a time, hidden under a thin hostel blanket.

After that first hour-long conversation, the floodgates opened. Our "casual" chats turned into something much more intense. We weren't just talking about his stories anymore; we were talking about everything.

My life became a cycle of checking my BSNL balance.

Back then, we didn't have unlimited data or WhatsApp calls. We had message packs—100 SMS a day if you were lucky, and the constant anxiety of seeing that "Low Balance" notification. I remember the routine of rushing to the small recharge shop near the hostel, handing over a crumpled note, and waiting for the confirmation message like it was a lifeline.

Once the lights in the hostel went out at 11:00 PM, my real day began.

I would pull the blanket over my head, creating a small, oxygen-deprived tent. In the suffocating heat of a 2014 April, with the ceiling fan creaking overhead, the only light in my world was the glowing screen of my keypad phone.

"Still awake?" his message would arrive, making the phone vibrate against my pillow.

"Trying to study. You?" I’d reply, though my textbook had been closed for hours.

"Thinking about that 'mesh house' story. Narayan Bhai just tripped over a bucket in the dark. The silence here is a lie."

I’d stifle a laugh, burying my face in the pillow so my roommate wouldn’t wake up. We would talk until the clock hit 3:00 AM, then 4:00 AM. We talked about silly, meaningless things—what the canteen’s "special tea" actually tasted like (burnt rubber), the movies we wanted to see, and how B.Tech felt like a four-year prank we were all playing on ourselves.

Sometimes, we’d just play games with words. He’d send a single sentence, and I’d have to finish the story.

It was exhausting. My eyes would burn the next morning in the 8:00 AM lecture, and I’d survive on caffeine and the thought of the next "ping." But I didn't care. There was a thrill in the secrecy of it.

Under that blanket, I wasn't a student with a "perfectly planned life" or a girl with her heart "anchored somewhere else." I was just a girl who felt understood by a boy who lived through words.

He knew my favorite song before he knew my favorite color. He knew what made me angry before he even knew how I looked when I was mad. We were building a world together, 160 characters at a time.

I remember one night, just as the birds were starting to chirp outside the hostel window, he sent:
"I think I’m running out of messages for the day. Save the last one for me?"

I stared at the screen, my thumb hovering over the keypad. My heart was racing, and it wasn't because of the lack of sleep.

"Okay," I typed. "Last one saved."

I fell asleep with the phone tucked under my chin. I felt safe. I felt seen. And I completely forgot that every story, no matter how beautiful, eventually has to face the light of day.

I didn't realize that by letting him into my 4:00 AM world, I was letting him into the parts of my heart I had promised to someone else.
